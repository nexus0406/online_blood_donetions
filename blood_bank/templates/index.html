Frequently Asked Questions

Most people in good health, at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some states), and weighing at least 110 pounds can donate blood. There are some additional eligibility requirements related to health, travel, and medications that will be reviewed before donation.

You can donate whole blood every 56 days (8 weeks). Platelet donations can be made more frequently - up to 24 times per year - with at least 7 days between donations.

Yes, donating blood is safe. New, sterile disposable equipment is used for each donor, so there's no risk of contracting a bloodborne infection by donating blood.

The entire process takes about an hour. The actual donation of a pint of whole blood unit takes 8-10 minutes. The process includes registration, a brief medical screening, donation, and refreshments.

Get a good night's sleep, eat a healthy meal before donating, drink extra fluids (avoid caffeine), and bring your donor card, driver's license, or other form of ID.
